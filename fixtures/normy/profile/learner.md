# Sam — how I work

## How I Learn

I learn by doing it and messing it up. reading a recipe doesn't stick — I have to have my hands in the dough and feel it go wrong before it means anything. I take notes right after a bake while it's still fresh, because if I wait until tomorrow I've already forgotten what I changed.

## Where I Get Stuck

impatience, mostly. I rush the proof because I want to see the result now, and rushing is the exact thing that ruins it. I also trust the clock over my own eyes, even when the dough is clearly telling me something different. I'd rather follow a number than admit I have to wait and pay attention.

## Why I Care

I want to make something with my hands that's alive and a little bit out of my control. a good loaf feels like small proof that slowing down actually works — that if I pay attention, the thing comes out right. and honestly I just love how the kitchen smells on a slow Sunday morning.
