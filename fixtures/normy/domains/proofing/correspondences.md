# proofing — how it connects

## To Crumb

the crumb is just the proofing made visible. when I cut a loaf open, the holes are a photograph of how the fermentation went — big even holes mean the gas was there and I caught it at the right moment, tight and gummy means I rushed or waited too long.

## To Starter

everything about proofing traces back to how awake the starter was when I mixed. a lively starter proofs on schedule; a tired one drags and throws my whole read of the dough off.
